# Gaming Tables/Desks Competitive Matching Guide

> **CRITICAL INSTRUCTION:** Before assessing this product, you MUST check `parity_rules.md` to define the strict Dealbreakers (Critical vs Variable specs). The points and rules below are for **qualitative context** only. Do NOT use them to define what is strict vs variable.

## 1. Qualitative Context & "Product Soul"

### Motorized Height Adjustment (20 points)
**Definition:** Electric standing desk vs fixed-height desk.

**Values:**
- `fixed` - Fixed height (standard desk)
- `manual` - Manual crank adjustment
- `motorized` - Electric motor height adjustment (standing desk)

**Why Critical:** Motorized standing desk vs fixed desk = completely different product category.

**Rejection Rule:**
- Motorized ≠ Fixed → **AUTO-REJECT**
- Motorized vs manual = acceptable (same adjustable category)

**Key Insight:** Customer shopping for standing desk will NOT accept fixed desk. Different health/ergonomic goals.

---

### Price/Build Tier (15 points)
**Definition:** The market segment and material quality.

**Rejection Rule:**
- **Budget (<$100)** vs **Premium (>$400)** = **AUTO-REJECT**
- Example: Particle board $80 desk vs Solid Wood/Steel $500 desk.
- **Why:** Material durability and stability are vastly different.
- **Tolerance:** Neighboring tiers OK. Budget vs High-End is NOT.

### Monitor Arm Compatibility (10 points)
**Definition:** Desktop edge thickness and material strength for clamping monitor arms.

**Values:**
- `incompatible` - Particle board or thin edge (<18mm), will crack/warp
- `clamp_compatible` - Solid edge 18-25mm, can support clamp-style arms
- `premium_compatible` - Thick edge 25mm+, solid wood/MDF, supports heavy dual arms

**Why Critical:** Many productivity/gaming users require monitor arms.
- Particle board desks CANNOT support monitor arm clamps (edge crumbles)
- Thin edges (<18mm) crack under clamp pressure
- Monitor arms require 10-80mm edge thickness clearance

**Rejection Rule:**
- If target supports monitor arms, candidate must have compatible edge
- Incompatible vs Compatible = **REJECT** for productivity/multi-monitor setups

**Functional Impact:**
- Single monitor arm: ~5kg clamping force
- Dual monitor arm: ~10kg clamping force
- Particle board fails under sustained clamping

---

### Size Class (10 points)
**Definition:** Desktop dimensions (length × width).

**Classes:**
- `compact_<120cm` - Small desks (<47")
- `standard_120-150cm` - Standard gaming desks (47-59")
- `large_150-180cm` - Large desks (59-71")
- `ultra_wide_>180cm` - Massive desks (>71")

**Why Critical:** Desk size defines room space and monitor setup capacity.
- Compact (1-monitor setup) ≠ Ultra-wide (3-monitor setup)

**Rejection Rule:**
- Must be within same class or adjacent
- Compact vs Ultra-wide = **REJECT**
- Standard vs Large = acceptable

**Tolerance:** ±20cm within class acceptable

---

## 2. Structural & Quality Features

### Weight Capacity (20 points)
**Acceptable Range:** Within same tier

**Tiers:**
- `light_<50kg` - Budget desks
- `standard_50-100kg` - Gaming desks
- `heavy_duty_>100kg` - Workstation/multi-monitor setups

**Logic:** Weight capacity indicates build quality and target use case.

**Tolerance:** ±25% acceptable

---

### Material Quality (10 points)
**Definition:** Desktop material (particle board, MDF, solid wood, steel).

**Logic:** Particle board = budget. Solid wood/steel = premium. Match quality tier.

## 3. Convenience Features

### RGB Lighting (10 points)
**Definition:** Integrated RGB strips or lighting.

**Logic:** Gaming aesthetic feature, not functional. Not critical.

---

### Cable Management (5 points)
**Definition:** Built-in cable trays, grommets, or channels.

**Logic:** Nice-to-have organization feature.

---


### Cable Management Infrastructure (15 points)
**Definition:** How the desk handles wires.

**Tiers:**
- `basic` - Grommets only.
- `advanced` - Full length trays / nets.
- `integrated_power` - **Power Column** / Built-in Power Delivery (Secretlab Magnus Pro).

**Why Critical:**
- A user buying a Magnus Pro ($800) is buying the *clean setup*, not just the desk.
- **Rejection Rule:**
- Integrated Power Column is a **Unique Selling Point**.
- Standard tray desks are NOT direct competitors to Magnus Pro unless price is the only factor (which we ignore now).

---

### Accessory Hooks/Holders (5 points)
**Definition:** Headphone hooks, cup holders, controller stands.

**Logic:** Gaming convenience features.

## 5. Verification Checklist

### Pre-Search
- [ ] Identify if motorized standing desk or fixed desk **CRITICAL**
- [ ] Determine size class (compact/standard/large/ultra-wide)
- [ ] Note weight capacity requirement (50kg, 100kg, etc.)
- [ ] Check material preference (budget vs premium)

### During Search
- [ ] **Verify height adjustment type** - Fixed, manual, or motorized?
- [ ] **Measure desktop dimensions** - Length × Width
- [ ] **Check weight capacity** - Can it support multi-monitor setup?
- [ ] **Read material specs** - Particle board, MDF, solid wood?
- [ ] Check for RGB, cable management, accessory hooks

### Customer Persona Test
**Question:** *"Customer wants 140cm motorized standing desk. Would they accept 120cm fixed desk?"*

**Answer:** **NO** - Different desk type (standing vs fixed) AND different size class.

## 6. Reasoning Examples

### ✅ GOOD MATCH: Cougar Mars 150 vs Eureka Ergonomic Z1-S

**Target: Cougar Mars 150**
- Type: Fixed height gaming desk
- Size: 150cm × 75cm
- Weight Capacity: 80kg
- RGB: Yes (edge lighting)
- Material: Carbon fiber texture, steel frame

**Candidate: Eureka Ergonomic Z1-S 140cm**
- Type: Fixed height gaming desk
- Size: 140cm × 60cm
- Weight Capacity: 90kg
- RGB: Yes (LED strip)
- Material: PVC surface, steel frame

**Analysis:**
- **CRITICAL - Type:** ✅ Both fixed height gaming desks
- **CRITICAL - Size:** ⚠️ 150cm vs 140cm (10cm diff, within tolerance)
- **STRUCTURAL - Weight:** ✅ 80kg vs 90kg (similar tier)
- **CONVENIENCE - RGB:** ✅ Both have RGB
- **Material:** ✅ Both gaming-grade materials

**Match Score:** 92/100

**Why It's a Match:** Both fixed-height gaming desks in standard size class with RGB and similar weight capacity.

---

### ❌ BAD MATCH: Fixed Gaming Desk vs Motorized Standing Desk

**Target: Budget Fixed Gaming Desk**
- Type: Fixed height
- Size: 120cm × 60cm

**Candidate: FlexiSpot E7 Motorized Standing Desk**
- Type: Motorized electric standing desk
- Size: 140cm × 70cm
- Height Range: 60-125cm (adjustable)

**Analysis:**
- **CRITICAL - Type:** ❌ Fixed vs Motorized = **completely different products**

**Why It's NOT a Match:** Customer buying fixed gaming desk doesn't need standing desk functionality. Different product categories.

## 8. Market Tier Indicators

### Budget (<$200)
- Fixed height
- Compact to standard size (100-140cm)
- 50-80kg weight capacity
- Particle board or basic MDF
- Basic RGB or no RGB
- Minimal cable management
- Brands: Generic, regional brands

### Mid-Range ($200-400)
- Fixed height gaming desks
- Standard to large size (140-160cm)
- 80-120kg weight capacity
- Carbon fiber texture, premium MDF
- RGB lighting standard
- Cable management trays
- Gaming accessories (headphone hook, cup holder)
- Brands: Cougar, Eureka Ergonomic, AKRacing

### Premium (>$400)
- Motorized standing desks (electric height adjustment)
- Large to ultra-wide (160-200cm)
- 100-150kg weight capacity
- Solid wood, bamboo, or premium materials
- Advanced cable management
- Memory presets (for standing desks)
- Premium brands: Secretlab, FlexiSpot, Autonomous
