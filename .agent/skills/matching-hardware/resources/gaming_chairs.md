# Gaming Chairs Competitive Matching Guide

> **CRITICAL INSTRUCTION:** Before assessing this product, you MUST check `parity_rules.md` to define the strict Dealbreakers (Critical vs Variable specs). The points and rules below are for **qualitative context** only. Do NOT use them to define what is strict vs variable.

## 1. Qualitative Context & "Product Soul"

### Chair Style / Design Philosophy (20 points)
**Definition:** The fundamental design approach and target market.

**Values:**
- `racing_bucket_seat` - Gaming racing style with wings, aggressive aesthetics
- `ergonomic_office` - Office chair with mesh back, ergonomic focus
- `executive_leather` - High-back leather executive style
- `minimalist_task` - Simple task chair, clean design

**Why Critical:** Racing vs Ergonomic = COMPLETELY DIFFERENT BUYER PERSONAS.
- Racing chair buyer wants gaming aesthetic, bold colors, "cool" factor
- Ergonomic office buyer wants professional look, breathability, lumbar support
- These markets DO NOT overlap

**Rejection Rule:**
- Racing ≠ Ergonomic ≠ Executive → **AUTO-REJECT**
- Customer wanting racing chair will REJECT mesh ergonomic chair (looks "boring")
- Customer wanting ergonomic chair will REJECT racing chair (looks "childish")

**Key Insight:** This is THE most important criterion. Get this wrong = total mismatch.

---


### User Size Capability (15 points)
**Definition:** The height/weight range the chair is physically built for.

**Tiers:**
- `small` - <170cm (Secretlab Small, Razer Iskur)
- `standard` - 170-185cm (Standard models)
- `XL/Heavy` - >185cm / >120kg (Titan XL, Hero, Iskur XL)

**Rejection Rule:**
- **Standard** vs **XL** = **AUTO-REJECT**.
- **Reason:** A physical mismatch. An XL user cannot sit in a Standard chair (too narrow). A Small user swims in an XL chair.
- **Strict Parity:** Match Size Class EXACTLY.
- **Ignore Price:** If the only XL competitor is $200 more, it is still the match.


### Recline Range (10 points)
**Definition:** Maximum recline angle from vertical.

**Tiers:**
- `fixed_90-120°` - Basic office chairs
- `gaming_135-155°` - Gaming recline (nap-capable)
- `extreme_>170°` - Full-flat gaming chairs

**Why Critical:** Recline defines use case.
- Fixed 120° = office work only
- 155° = gaming sessions + napping
- 170°+ = streamer/extreme gaming

**Rejection Rule:**
- Fixed office (120°) ≠ Gaming recline (155°) = different functionality
- 135° vs 155° = acceptable (both gaming tier)

**Tolerance:** ±15° within tier acceptable

---

### Weight Capacity (10 points)
**Definition:** Maximum supported user weight.

**Tiers:**
- `standard_<120kg` - Average build
- `heavy_duty_120-150kg` - Larger users
- `extreme_>150kg` - Bariatric/heavy-duty

**Why Critical:** Weight capacity is safety requirement.

**Rejection Rule:**
- Must meet or exceed target weight capacity
- Underspec = **REJECT** (safety risk)
- Overspec = acceptable

## 2. Structural & Comfort Features

### Upholstery Material (15 points)
**Definition:** Primary covering material.

**Values:**
- `PU_leather` - Polyurethane faux leather (most gaming chairs)
- `PVC_leather` - Vinyl leather (budget)
- `fabric_mesh` - Breathable mesh (ergonomic chairs)
- `genuine_leather` - Real leather (executive chairs)

**Logic:** Material affects breathability, durability, aesthetics.
- PU leather (gaming) vs Mesh (ergonomic) = different cooling properties
- Mesh = breathable, hot climates
- PU leather = aesthetics, cold climates

**Tolerance:** Within same category acceptable (PU vs PVC both leather-style)

---

### Armrest Adjustability (10 points)
**Definition:** Degrees of freedom for armrest adjustment.

**Tiers:**
- `fixed` - No adjustment
- `1D_height` - Height only
- `2D_height_width` - Height + width
- `3D_height_width_depth` - Height + width + depth
- `4D_all_directions` - Height + width + depth + angle

**Logic:** More adjustment = better ergonomics.

**Tolerance:** More adjustability = upgrade. Less = downgrade.

---

### Lumbar Support Type (5 points)
**Definition:** How lumbar support is provided.

**Values:**
- `none` - No lumbar support
- `pillow` - Removable lumbar pillow
- `built_in_fixed` - Fixed built-in lumbar curve
- `adjustable_mechanical` - Height-adjustable lumbar mechanism (Secretlab Titan, Razer Iskur V2)

**Why Critical:**
- A user buying a "State of the Art" chair (Titan/Iskur V2) wants the **Integrated Lumbar**.
- **Rejection Rule:**
- Integrated Mechanical Lumbar ≠ Pillow.
- **Auto-Reject** if matching a high-end Integrated Lumbar chair with a Pillow chair. They are different technology tiers.

**Logic:** Lumbar support is ergonomic requirement for long sessions.

**Tolerance:** Any lumbar support better than none. Adjustable better than fixed.

## 3. Convenience Features

### Tilt Mechanism (10 points)
**Definition:** Seat tilt locking functionality.

**Values:**
- `fixed` - No tilt
- `basic_tilt_lock` - Lock at specific angles
- `infinite_tilt` - Lock at any angle
- `synchro_tilt` - Seat + backrest tilt together (ergonomic)

**Logic:** Tilt mechanism affects comfort but not critical for gaming chairs.

---

### Footrest (5 points)
**Definition:** Retractable or built-in footrest.

**Logic:** Nice-to-have feature for recline comfort. Not dealbreaker.

---

### Headrest/Neck Pillow (5 points)
**Definition:** Removable or built-in headrest pillow.

**Logic:** Common on gaming chairs, comfort feature.

---

- [ ] Check recline range tier (office/gaming/extreme) **CRITICAL**
- [ ] Verify weight capacity requirement **CRITICAL**

## 7. Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Mistake 1: "Matching racing chairs to ergonomic chairs"
**Why Wrong:** These serve different buyer personas. Racing = gaming aesthetic. Ergonomic = professional minimalist.

**Fix:** Racing chairs only match racing chairs. Ergonomic only match ergonomic.

---

### ❌ Mistake 2: "Ignoring recline range"
**Why Wrong:** Fixed 120° office chair vs 165° gaming recliner = different use cases.

**Fix:** Match recline tiers (office/gaming/extreme).

---

### ❌ Mistake 3: "Matching by weight capacity alone"
**Why Wrong:** 130kg basic chair vs 130kg premium chair with 4D armrests = different tiers.

**Fix:** Verify chair style, material, and features first. Weight capacity is secondary.

---

## 8. Technical Tier Indicators

### Entry/Budget Tier
**Technical Signals:**
- PVC or basic PU leather
- 90-135° recline
- 1D or 2D armrests
- Removable lumbar pillow
- Nylon base
- Standard plastic casters
- 100-120kg weight capacity

**Common Use:** Casual gaming, budget home office

---

### Mid-Range Tier
**Technical Signals:**
- Quality PU leather or fabric
- 135-155° recline with locks
- 3D or 4D armrests
- Built-in or adjustable lumbar
- Metal or reinforced base
- Rubber-coated or rollerblade casters
- 120-150kg weight capacity
- Optional footrest

**Common Use:** Regular gaming, home office, streaming

---

### Premium Tier
**Technical Signals:**
- Premium synthetic leather, genuine leather, or advanced mesh
- 155-170°+ recline with infinite lock
- 4D armrests (full adjustability)
- Advanced lumbar support system (magnetic, mechanical)
- Aluminum or steel base
- Rollerblade wheels
- 130-180kg weight capacity
- Multi-year warranty
- Branded (Secretlab, Herman Miller, Noblechairs, Razer)

**Common Use:** Professional gaming, esports, executive office, ergonomic workstations
